3.1 Areas of Research/Scholarly Interest

I am interested generally in applying machine learning and computational techniques to analysis and problem solving in the domain of natural, spoken language, both perception and production. I am currently pursuing an M.S. in Computer Science at Oregon Health and Science University, under the Center for Spoken Language Understanding. In addition to my coursework in machine learning and data science, through the mentorship of Dr. Alexander Kain, I've had the opportunity to assist with ongoing development of a system which integrates automatic speech recognition and deep learning techniques to automatically assess the pronunciation of children with speech sound disorders.
I also have experience in the field of auditory speech perception and psychoacoustics, and have been involved in several projects investigating how humans across age groups, with and without hearing loss, perceive aspects of spoken language under a variety of realistic conditions.
